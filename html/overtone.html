So, what is Overtone? What can you do with it?

All music is a sonic event through time. Schedule events with "at". Use temporal recursion with "apply-at", which lets a function schedule a future call to itself, at a specified time.

John "Jab'o" Starks Groove

(defn licking-stick-drums [nome]        
    (let [beat (nome)]
        ; hi-hat pattern
        (at (nome beat) (ch))
        (at (nome (+ 1 beat)) (oh))
        ; snare pattern
        (at (nome (+ 2 beat)) (snare))
        (at (subdivide (nome (+ 2 beat)) (nome (+ 4 beat)) 3) (snare))
        ; kick drum pattern
        (at (nome beat) (kick))
        (at (nome (+ 5 beat)) (kick))

        ;finally, recur in 8 beats. we won't get fined if we drop one,
        ; like James' band did!
        (apply-at (nome (+ 8 beat)) licking-stick-drums nome [])))

Model a guitar

(definst plucked-string [note 60 amp 0.8 dur 2 decay 30 coef 0.3 gate 1]
  (let [freq   (midicps note)
        noize  (* 0.8 (white-noise))
        dly    (/ 1.0 freq)
        plk    (pluck noize gate dly dly decay coef)
        dist   (distort plk)
        filt   (rlpf dist (* 12 freq) 0.6)
        clp    (clip2 filt 0.8)
        reverb (free-verb clp 0.4 0.8 0.2)]
    (* amp (env-gen (perc 0.0001 dur)) reverb)))
